# Innovation Aspects

## Novel Application

- **First-of-its-kind approach:** Application of electrocoagulation specifically for microplastic aggregation and removal
- **Emerging environmental challenge:** Addresses the growing global concern of microplastic pollution in water systems
- **Particle engineering:** Uses electromagnetic fields to manipulate microplastic surface properties, enabling mechanical filtration

## Technical Innovation

- **Low-cost solution:** Operates on simple 9V DC power source, making it accessible and affordable
- **Material optimization:** Successfully transitioned from corroding steel electrodes to durable graphite electrodes through iterative problem-solving
- **Passive aggregation mechanism:** No chemical coagulants required—relies solely on electrochemical charge neutralization
- **Multi-matrix versatility:** Tested across diverse water types (sterilized, tap, RO, PVC-contaminated, cosmetic microplastics)

## Scientific Rigor

- **Triple-validation approach:** Combined DLS, FTIR, and UV-VIS spectroscopy for comprehensive characterization
- **Quantitative performance metrics:** 47% particle size increase, 37% charge reduction—measurable, reproducible results
- **University collaboration:** Independent validation by Universiti Sains Malaysia engineering faculty
- **Evidence-based design:** Built upon peer-reviewed electrocoagulation research literature

## Practical Innovation

- **Integration-ready design:** Compatible with existing water treatment infrastructure
- **Scalability potential:** Bench-scale prototype demonstrates proof-of-concept for larger systems
- **Minimal operational complexity:** Simple electrode configuration, no continuous chemical input required
- **Target applications identified:** Water treatment plants, swimming pools, point-of-use filtration

## Student-Led Research

- **Secondary school innovation:** High school students conducting university-level research
- **Complete research cycle:** From literature review → prototype development → laboratory validation → data analysis
- **Collaborative model:** Bridged academic institutions (STAR + USM) to access advanced analytical tools
- **Documentation excellence:** Comprehensive methodology, transparent reporting of limitations

## Environmental Impact

- **Preventive approach:** Removes microplastics before human/environmental exposure
- **Sustainable technology:** Energy-efficient, uses non-toxic graphite electrodes
- **Addresses multiple pollution sources:** Effective against industrial PVC and cosmetic microbeads
- **Supports SDG goals:** Clean water and sanitation (SDG 6), responsible consumption (SDG 12)

## What Makes ElectroNet Different

| Traditional Filtration | ElectroNet Approach |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| Limited to particles >1 μm | Aggregates sub-micron particles into filterable size |
| Passive separation only | Active particle manipulation before filtration |
| No change to particle properties | Alters surface charge and size distribution |
| High-grade filters required | Enables conventional filters to work effectively |
| Chemical flocculants often needed | Chemical-free electrochemical process |
